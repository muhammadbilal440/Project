The Geographical Features of the District

Jhang Sadr, the administrative centre of the district, is located at 31.27° latitude and 72.33° longitude, sitting at an elevation of 158 meters above sea level. The city is situated on the east bank of the Chenab River, near its confluence with the Jhelum River at Trimmu Barrage, close to the town of Athara Hazari. This strategic position on the riverbank has historically shaped Jhang's landscape, agriculture, and culture. Jhang's geography is heavily influenced by its rivers. Apart from the Chenab and Jhelum, the Ravi River also touches the district's boundary near Ahmadpur Sial, enriching the fertile plains of the region. These rivers collectively form a crucial part of the Indus River system, contributing to Jhang's agricultural economy. The district features a blend of diverse landforms, including the expansive Thal Desert, known for its rolling sand dunes and semi-arid conditions. This desert stretches across parts of Jhang, presenting challenges for agriculture but also showcasing the resilience of its inhabitants. In terms of urban layout, the city of Maghiana lies on the edge of the highlands, providing a vantage point over the alluvial valley of the Chenab River. In contrast, the older part of Jhang is situated in the lowlands at the base of these highlands. Flood Vulnerability: Jhang has faced threats from floods, particularly in 2014, when the Chenab River swelled dangerously. However, the floodwaters were successfully redirected towards Athara Hazari, sparing the city from significant damage. The area's flood management infrastructure, such as the Trimmu Barrage, plays a critical role in safeguarding the city and its surrounding regions. Jhang experiences an arid to semi-arid climate, with scorching summers and mild winters. The rivers, coupled with the rich alluvial soil, support extensive agriculture, which forms the backbone of the local economy. The primary crops include wheat, sugarcane, cotton, and rice, benefiting from the irrigation provided by the Chenab and Jhelum rivers. The district's geography also includes lush green fields, interspersed with pockets of desert terrain, creating a striking contrast that defines Jhang's natural beauty. The rivers not only sustain agriculture but also offer opportunities for fishing and other livelihoods for the local population. Jhang's unique geographical position, flanked by rivers and deserts, makes it an intriguing blend of natural contrasts, contributing to its historical and cultural significance.
